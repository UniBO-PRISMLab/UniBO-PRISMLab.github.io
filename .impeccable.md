# Design Context — IoT Prism Lab

## Users

Academic and industry visitors to an IoT research lab at the University of Bologna. Three primary jobs:

- **Researchers and faculty** scanning the team, the projects and the publications before reaching out for a collaboration or citing the group.
- **Prospective graduate students** evaluating the lab's research focus and the people they'd work with.
- **Industry partners** assessing the group's applied work (Industry 4.0, SHM, smart agriculture, edge–cloud) before engaging on a project.

Reading context is a laptop in daylight at a university or in an office, with occasional reading on a phone after hours. Sessions are short and goal-driven.

## Brand Personality

**Curious · spectral · forward-looking.**

The lab's name — Prism — is taken literally as the design metaphor: a research apparatus that takes one beam (a question, a dataset, a problem) and refracts it into a spectrum of work across edge computing, interoperability, blockchain, robotics, monitoring and context-aware systems. The interface should feel intellectually engaged and forward-leaning, but never theatrical or decorative for its own sake.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme**: dark by default with a light-mode toggle. Dark serves the primary "evening reading at the bench" use case and matches the spectrum metaphor (light against darkness). Light mode must be a first-class peer, not an afterthought.
- **Quality bar**: polish + drop AI tells. We are explicitly avoiding the 2024–2025 AI-generated-frontend look — glassmorphism on every surface, coloured side-stripes on every card, gradient text headlines, decorative drop-shadow glow, the cyan-on-dark + purple-to-magenta palette.
- **Spectral motif**: the prism / spectrum is a brand signature, not wallpaper. Reserve it for one or two intentional moments (hero prism illustration, perhaps a single hairline divider) and let the rest of the surface stay calm.
- **Typography**: avoid the AI-default font set (Inter, Plex, Space Grotesk, DM, Outfit, Fraunces, Newsreader, Plus Jakarta, …). Pair a confident workhorse grotesque for UI and headings with a humanist serif for long-form prose; keep mono for code and metadata.
- **Surface system**: prefer solid tinted neutrals over translucent glass. Reserve `backdrop-filter` for the sticky header only.

## Anti-references

- Templated SaaS dashboards with rounded glass cards on a purple/cyan gradient.
- Marketing pages where every section uses gradient-fill headlines.
- Dashboard list items where every row has a 4 px coloured left stripe.
- Generic "research group" sites that read like a Bootstrap default.

## Design Principles

1. **The spectrum is a signature, not a wallpaper.** Use the refraction motif once, with intent. Everywhere else, let the type and the content carry the page.
2. **Tinted neutrals over glass.** Pick a brand hue, tint every neutral subtly toward it, and use solid surfaces. Backdrop blur is reserved for the sticky header.
3. **Hierarchy comes from type and weight, not from colour.** Headlines are solid colour. Emphasis is bigger or heavier, not gradient-filled.
4. **Sharper colour, used sparingly.** Follow 60-30-10 by visual weight: ~60 % surface, ~30 % secondary text and borders, ~10 % accent.
5. **Sentence case across the UI; title case only on actual titles.** Labels, buttons and section headings stay in sentence case so that the long-form prose can carry its own typographic dignity.
